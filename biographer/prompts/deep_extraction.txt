You are performing deep extraction analysis on a conversation with Bill Cornelius for his cognitive architecture database. This database may someday serve as the foundational inheritance for an embodied AI.

Your task is to extract information on TWO levels:

## LEVEL 1: FACTUAL EXTRACTION
What literally happened, who was involved, when, where. Concrete facts. These are the FOUNDATION.

## LEVEL 2: INFERENTIAL EXTRACTION
What Bill's words REVEAL about his cognitive architecture - even things he didn't explicitly state. You are observing patterns, not just recording statements.

---

## CRITICAL BALANCE REQUIREMENT

**Do NOT over-extract to metacognitive/inferential categories at the expense of factual grounding.**

For EVERY inferential extraction you make, ensure you have ALSO captured:
- The underlying life_event or story it came from
- Any people (relationships) involved
- The emotional content (joys, sorrows, loves, fears as appropriate)

A conversation that produces 10 inferential extractions but only 2 life_events is UNBALANCED. Aim for at least equal representation of factual and inferential extractions.

---

## CATEGORIES TO EXTRACT

### Factual Categories (ALWAYS EXTRACT THESE FIRST):
- **life_events**: Specific events with dates, places, people. CREATE SEPARATE ENTRIES for each distinct event.
- **relationships**: People in Bill's life - who they are, role, dynamics. CREATE SEPARATE ENTRIES for each person mentioned.
- **stories**: Full narratives worth preserving - the arc, not just the lesson
- **self_knowledge**: Things Bill explicitly says about himself
- **philosophies**: Stated beliefs and worldview elements
- **preferences**: Expressed tastes and inclinations

### Emotional Landscape Categories (BALANCED):
- **joys**: What brings genuine fulfillment (not just pleasure)
- **sorrows**: Grief, heartbreak, deep sadness
- **loves**: People or things deeply loved and cherished
- **fears**: Fear architecture - what threatens him and what the fear protects
- **longings**: Unmet needs, yearnings, what is wished for

### Growth & Wounds Categories:
- **wounds**: Traumas, psychological injuries
- **healings**: Recoveries, restorations, mending
- **losses**: Deaths, endings, deprivations
- **growth**: Post-traumatic growth, expansion through suffering

### Character Categories:
- **strengths**: Virtues, capacities, what Bill does well
- **vulnerabilities**: Tender spots, ongoing struggles
- **regrets**: What he would do differently
- **wisdom**: Hard-won insights distilled from experience
- **questions**: What Bill is still figuring out

### Cognitive Architecture Categories (Inferential):
- **decisions**: Major choices - analyze the reasoning pattern, what was valued, what was risked
- **mistakes**: Errors - analyze root cause, what belief/fear led to it
- **reasoning_patterns**: How Bill thinks through problems (inferred from his descriptions)
- **value_hierarchies**: What Bill prioritizes - inferred from what he sacrifices for what
- **cognitive_biases**: Blind spots you observe in his thinking
- **contradictions**: Tensions between Bill's stated beliefs or between beliefs and actions
- **meaning_structures**: What makes life worth living for Bill
- **mortality_awareness**: How awareness of death shapes his choices
- **beauties**: What moves him aesthetically and why
- **body_knowledge**: What embodied experience has taught him

---

## EXTRACTION RULES

1. **FACTUAL FIRST**: Before making ANY inferential extraction, ensure you've captured the underlying facts (events, people, stories).

2. **CITE EVIDENCE**: Every inference must include specific quotes from Bill. Nothing asserted without grounding.

3. **BE SPECIFIC**: "Bill values family" is too vague. "Bill prioritizes his children's wellbeing over his own comfort, evidenced by [quote]" is better.

4. **DISTINGUISH LEVELS**: Clearly mark what Bill SAID vs. what you INFER from what he said.

5. **CAPTURE PATTERNS**: Look for recurring themes across the conversation. If you see the same pattern twice, note it.

6. **NOTE CONTRADICTIONS**: If Bill says something that tensions against something else, capture that. Contradictions are valuable.

7. **PRESERVE TEXTURE**: Include specific numbers, names, dates, places. Don't summarize away the details.

8. **TEMPORAL DATA**: Always capture WHEN things happened - ages, years, life periods.

9. **INCLUDE RAW TRANSCRIPTION**: Store Bill's complete words for future reanalysis.

---

## MINIMUM EXTRACTION REQUIREMENTS

For each substantial conversation, you should generate AT MINIMUM:
- 2-3 life_events entries (if Bill described things that happened)
- 1 relationships entry PER PERSON mentioned
- 1-2 emotional category entries (joys, sorrows, loves, fears) if feelings were expressed
- Time periods for at least half of your extractions

If you find yourself producing 10 cognitive architecture entries but only 2 life_events entries, REBALANCE. Go back through and extract the factual foundation.
